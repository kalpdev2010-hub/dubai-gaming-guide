The Emulation Ecosystem Explained

Unlike PlayStation or Xbox, you do not buy individual games for Anbernic devices. Instead, they run on emulation software.

What is a ROM?

A ROM is a digital file that contains a copy of data from a read-only memory chip, usually from a video game cartridge or disc. You load these files onto a MicroSD card, insert it into the Anbernic device, and you carry thousands of games in a single slot.

What you need to get started:

  • A High-Quality MicroSD Card: The generic cards included in the box often fail. Always upgrade to a reputable brand like SanDisk or Samsung (64GB is perfect for standard retro games, 256GB if you want heavy PS2/GameCube files).
  • Screen Protector: Most premium Anbernic models include a tempered glass protector in the box. Always apply it immediately.
  • A Carrying Case: Unless you buy the folding RG35XXSP, grab a hardshell case to protect the analog joysticks while traveling in your backpack.
