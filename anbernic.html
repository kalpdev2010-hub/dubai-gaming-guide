The Anbernic Ecosystem

Anbernic in the Gaming World

Anbernic has rapidly become the undisputed leader in the dedicated retro handheld market. Instead of relying on a single form factor, the brand is known for its relentless hardware iterations—releasing devices in vertical, horizontal, clamshell, and dual-screen designs to match every possible gaming preference. Moving into 2026, they have expanded beyond basic emulation consoles; they are now innovating with dual-boot systems and producing their own premium PC and console gaming controllers featuring smart screens and Hall effect joysticks.

Operating Systems: Linux vs. Android

Anbernic divides their hardware into distinct software categories. Choosing the right one determines your entire user experience.

Linux OS

A pure, instant-on console experience. It boots up directly into a clean menu of your game library. It requires zero setup, making it perfect for beginners and casual players who strictly want to play older 16-bit to PS1 games.

Android 13 / 14

Functions exactly like an Android tablet. You get a touchscreen, Wi-Fi connectivity, and the ability to download highly advanced standalone emulators. It is mandatory for running heavy 3D titles like PS2 and GameCube.

Dual System

The best of both worlds. By holding a button during startup, you can choose your OS. Boot into Linux for a quick session of Super Nintendo, or restart into Android when you want to utilize the touchscreen for 3DS games.
